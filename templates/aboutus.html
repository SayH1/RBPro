About Us


Health is one of the most important concerns that everyone has in mind. Including sickness and diseases, humans try everything to avoid and prevent these afflictions. Cancer is yet another one of the diseases that is a cause for alarm. One of the most important cancer types, yet lacking in attention is Retinoblastoma or RB for short, a disease that usually manifests in newborns ranging from infants to children within the age of 5 years old. If the disease is left untreated, the child is expected to have a life expectancy around 1-2 years.

Nowadays, there are many various ways to treat Retinoblastoma. However, every method can cause all kinds of side effects, including permanent side effects. Due to the aforementioned side effects, our team has realized the importance of dealing with this problem. As a result, our team has decided to start the project “RB-Pro: Software Program to Screen and Predict Potential Active Compound to Treat Retinoblastoma in Childhood Patients Using Computational Techniques in Precision Medicine”. With precision medicine, it allows for a more precise and personalized treatment. It can be done by gaining more knowledge about each patient by analyzing their genes to select candidate targets for a drug to tackle precisely with minimal side effects.

This project consists of different modules including Genome Analysis, Pathway Analysis, Compound Analysis, Feature Identification Analysis, Model Prediction, and Results Visualization. Whereas, all of these functions would be useful to researchers, drug developers, and scientists by providing gene analysis software with diverse visualizations, active compound screening and predicting, all of which are expected to aid in decision making processes. Ultimately, the project can push the medical field to the next level, and be of service to humanity.
